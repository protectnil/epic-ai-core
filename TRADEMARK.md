Ensuring proper use of the Epic AI® mark is essential to maintaining the clear association between the mark and the quality, security, and reliability for which Epic AI® products are known. ## Nominative Fair Use

To accurately identify Epic AI® products, it may be necessary to refer to them by name. The basic rules for nominative fair use are: be accurate and not misleading, use only the word mark (not logos), and do not use the mark more prominently or frequently than necessary.

Examples of permitted nominative fair uses:

- "You can download the Epic AI® Legion source code under the Apache License 2.0 at https://github.com/protectnil/epic-ai-legion."
- "Our product is compatible with @epicai/legion."
- "I recommend Epic AI® Legion for federated enterprise orchestration."

Use of the Epic AI® name in any nominative context does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation with protectNIL Inc.

## Using Epic AI® Trademarks in Product Branding

You may not use Epic AI® trademarks in your own product name. You may use the phrase "Compatible With Epic AI® Legion" in product descriptions, provided you also follow the rest of this policy. Use of "Powered By," "for the Epic AI® platform," or similar conventions that imply a closer relationship requires prior written permission from protectNIL Inc.

## Derivative Works

The Apache License 2.0 allows you to create forks and derivative works of Epic AI® Legion. However, you must follow these trademark guidelines:

- **Use a different name.** You may not call your derivative work "Epic AI" or use any confusingly similar name, including variations in spacing, punctuation, or spelling. You may refer to it only as "a derivative work of," "derived from," or "compatible with" Epic AI® Legion.
- **Remove all branding.** Remove the Epic AI® logo, hexagon device, and any associated trade dress from your distribution.
- **Provide clear attribution.** State that your version is modified and not endorsed by protectNIL Inc.

## Logos and Visual Marks

The Epic AI® logo and hexagon device are not licensed under the Apache License 2.0. You may not use, reproduce, or create derivative works of Epic AI® logos, or adopt confusingly similar visual marks, without written permission from protectNIL Inc.

## Domain Names and Social Media

You may not use Epic AI® trademarks in domain names, social media account names, or handles without written permission from protectNIL Inc. Use of trademarks in domain names is not considered nominative fair use because the domain name refers to your offerings, not merely to our products.

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses violate this policy:

- Branding products or services in a manner confusingly similar to Epic AI® branding
- Examples of prohibited names include, but are not limited to: EpicAI, Epic-AI, Epic AI Core Pro, Epic IVA, or any similar variation
- Providing software or services consisting of Epic AI® software under alternative names that incorporate the Epic AI® mark
- Using company or product names that are confusingly similar to Epic AI®, including in appearance, sound, meaning, or overall commercial impression
- Claiming protectNIL Inc. approval or endorsement without a written agreement
